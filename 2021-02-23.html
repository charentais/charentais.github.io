Is it really worth growing brassicas?
Kale in frost.


Brassicas include broccoli, cabbage, Brussels sprouts, kale, cauliflower, bok choi (pak choi), mustard, watercress, rocket (arugula), turnip, swede, radish and land cress.

Nutrition and health benefits.
Brassicas contain vitamins C, A, E and K. They also contain folate, calcium, iron, potassium and phosphorus. They are a good source of fibre and glucosinolates (sulphur containing compounds).

Pros
A winter hardy vegetable. Brassicas don’t grow when it’s cold but they don’t keel over either. They just sit there enjoying the cold and frost. Some varieties of kale will put on new growth when the weather begins to warm in the spring.
Grow your own Brussels sprouts for Christmas. What could be better than your own Brussels sprouts that you loved so much as a child.
Brussels sprouts are an incredible looking plant, and so are cauliflower with their brain like curds.
Purple sprouting broccoli supplies fresh greens in late winter. What a plant they are too, with their seemingly endless supply of new shoots.

Cons
Brassicas require netting. And netting is a pain. I use plant pots on top of canes to support the net. The wind keeps blowing the pots off. The net has to be lifted for access. If you don’t net you’re likely to get pigeon attacks and if you don’t use a fine mesh net you will likely get problems with the cabbage white butterfly.

Brassica diseases
Cabbage root fly can take your beloved brassicas out at the base. They lay their eggs at the base of the plant and the hatching larvae will eat away at the roots. Biodegradeable collars can be placed around the base of the stem to prevent the problem.
Cabbage white butterfly lay their yellow eggs on the leaves of brassica plants. The eggs hatch into caterpillars and they feed on the leaves. A bad infestation will cover them in holes. A well secured fine mesh net will deter the butterfly.
Club root is caused by a soil borne fungus. I thought that once the soil is infected with club root you can wave goodbye to growing brassicas on the same site for 20 years. However it may be possible to still succeed with brassicas if for example the plants are started in modules and planted out when they get to a good size. Good hygiene will help. Don’t compost any brassica remains.

Other issues
Soil ph. They are particular about ph requirements. Liming is often recommended when growing brassicas.
Health precautions: Possible effects on the thyroid. Difficult to digest for some people. That sulphur taste.
Long growing season. PSB for example can be in the ground for 12 months.
Children don’t like brassicas. Can you blame them? Brassicas have a bitter taste due to the glucosinolates.

Who could be bothered?

So is it worth growing brassicas?
Of course it is. I am going to make a wooden frame to fit over the raised beds which will be covered in fine mesh netting to keep out pigeons and cabbage white butterflies. The structure can be moved around the raised beds following the crop rotation. I haven’t yet encountered club root and the other issues are easily managed. Best of luck to you if you choose to grow brassicas.
