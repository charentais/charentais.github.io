Winter lettuce
Brun d'hiver in the greenhouse.


I sowed some Brun d’Hiver lettuce seeds in mid July in cells. I transplanted them in mid August to a raised bed. The remainder of the lettuce seedlings were put into the tomato pots in the greenhouse when the tomatoes were cut down in September. The outdoor lettuce plants grow until it gets too cold and the light levels diminish. Then they just sit there waiting for the temperature to increase but I do collect a few lettuce leaves. The greenhouse plants keep growing due to better conditions but do slow down in the depths of winter. I get more lettuce from the greenhouse plants to use as a supplement to supermarket lettuce. As the weather warms up they will produce more leaves before eventually going to seed. This way we get lettuce in early spring before the new season plantings are ready. So a bit of work at the end of the season reaps some rewards at the beginning of the new season.

Brun d'hiver outside.

Brun d’hiver A cold hardy winter butterhead lettuce introduced in 1855. The green leaves are tinged with red/ brown. Nice, soft leaves compared to the tough supermarket varieties.
