Juice a Pomegranate
Pomegranates are difficult to eat with all those fibrous seeds. Fortunately there is an easy way to juice a pomegranate. Take your fresh ripe pomegranate and press on it all over, firmly but gently. Roll it around on your cutting board. Stare at it hard. Think juice not fibre. After you’ve given it a good going over, grab a sharp knife and cut the protruding top off level with the main body. This will reveal the top but you’re not quite there yet. Now gently insert the blade of the knife into the top and cut a small hole. Invert the pomegranate over a glass. If you do it right you should get a decent amount of delicious pomegranate juice. If you do it wrong you may you may have to spend some time cleaning up your kitchen. You can cut into the fruit to get any last remaining deliciousness. This method gets some of the juice out but to extract more a blender or processor will come in handy. Scrape the seeds into the blender. Blend for about 10-20 seconds and strain over a glass or jug to get the rest of the juice out. The taste is both sweet and astringent. The deep red colour of the juice looks like a fine red wine.
