Grow pea shoots
Compost on the left, paper towel on the right.

Pea shoots are a great vegetable to start early in the spring to give some fresh shoots right from your kitchen windowsill.

Pea varieties.
I would try any variety you have to hand. If you have to buy them specially go for whatever grabs you. I often grow Kelvedon Wonder in the spring so this is the variety I have used in the past. This year germination wasn’t good so I got hold of some dried peas from the supermarket. 500g for less than £1.00. Much cheaper than going for special seed varieties. You could soak the seeds for about 8 hours before planting to help get them going faster.

Grab a module tray or seed tray and part fill with fresh compost. I plant lots of seeds and cover with 2 - 3 cm of compost. Water well and loosely cover with a plastic bag or similar. Place in a light spot such as a windowsill or greenhouse. I have tried growing them in the top of a small tomato house and this worked fine. I also tried them in an unheated greenhouse and the seeds were stolen, probably by mice but I didn’t actually observe the raider. I even tried covering the seed tray with a clear propagator top and the thief managed to move the top aside and again stole the pea seeds!

Make sure the compost doesn’t dry out. Remove the bag once the seeds begin to germinate. Water as neccesary and begin to harvest the fresh shoots when they get to a decent size (about 15cm tall). Leave a 5cm stub when cutting and they should regrow again.

I also tried a half seed tray using a few layers of paper towel for a comparison. I used 50g of dry peas that were then soaked and spread out on the kitchen towel and then covered for a few days to start the germination in the dark. Expose to the light once the germination has started and the shoots will begin to grow towards the light. Keep watered. I used filtered water and alternated with a weak seaweed solution.

Comparison between growing in compost and on paper towel.
When growing on paper towel the shoots seem to grow sideways but then they start to grow upright. Germination wasn’t too high and so the pea shoots were a bit sparse. Apart from this they seemed to be growing well with just some nutrients from the seaweed solution. However when comparing side by side, the pea shoots growing in compost are doing better. The leaves are larger and they look very healthy. By the end of week 4 the peas shoots grown on paper towel are looking kind of tired whereas the pea shoots grown in compost are thriving.

Pea shoots are a great addition to a salad. The freshness and taste is fantastic at this time of year.

Pea shoots are a good source of beta carotene, vitamin C, folate and fibre. They are also low in fat and carbohydrates but higher in protein for all you protein monsters out there.

Green salad with pea shoots
courgette, cucumber, celery, pear, pea shoots. Cut and shred some courgette and cucumber. Add sliced celery and pear. Add some chopped parsley and other herbs if you wish. Finish with the pea shoots and some lemon juice and maybe even a spoonful of tahini. No measuring in this recipe. Do what you want.
