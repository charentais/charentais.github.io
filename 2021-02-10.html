Pick and cut a mango
Mango


Mangos hit peak season in the summer but they are available all year round coming in from various different countries. There are hundreds of varieties of mangos but the usual varieties in the UK are Kent, Tommy Atkins, Keitt, and Palmer. There are also some minor varieties such as Alphonso and Kesar which are sometimes available in peak season and the Osteen mango from Spain.

Mango quality varies. When you hit a good one it is Devine. A bad one sucks. I always prefer to go for a mango that has some give when pressed. If they are really soft they can also be good but sometimes when you cut into them the flesh is beginning to rot. I avoid mangos that are rock hard as they often don’t ripen well and again when they finally soften they can be bad inside.

Some fruits are easy to get into and others are just a pain. Mangos are delicious but sometimes it feels like they just don’t wanna give up their fruit. The stone is huge, in fact it runs the whole length of the fruit. You can buy a mango splitter if you wish. I haven’t tried one as I prefer to use a knife and reduce the amount of gadgets I have in the kitchen.

Okay, so cut round the stalk first and discard it. Then sit the mango on the stalk end. Get your knife and judge roughly where the huge stone is. Cut first round one side from the top to the bottom keeping your knife close to the stone. Free the first section and then repeat on the opposite side. So, that part is fairly easy and you can use a knife to score into the flesh of the two sides and scoop out the mango, or invert the fruit by pushing out from behind. The real test comes next when you try and tackle the inner section. After cutting away the 2 sides we are left with skin, flesh and stone. First cut away the skin right round the fruit and scrape the flesh from the skin. Next cut into the sides of the fruit that are attached to the stone and cut this away. It’s often difficult and the mango acts like a slippery fish when trying to accomplish this task. A spoon may also be useful to scrape the flesh from the stone. The last resort is to suck at what’s left using your mouth. By the end of the process you should be left with a delectable amount of fruit and mango juice all over your mouth, hands, cutting board and floor.

Let’s hope it’s a good mango because you deserve a great reward after all that hard work.
